# GKOS governance

## Purpose

This document governs amendment, review, publication, and repository authority during the pre-v1.0 development period and identifies the governance work required before GKOS v1.0.

## Development-phase authority

Shaun “Oden” Marshall serves as Founder and Initial Editor during the v0.x concept-testing and refinement phase.

A v0.x change may be adopted by the Founder and Initial Editor after documented technical review, compatibility analysis, and repository validation. Such an adoption is a **development decision**, not a consensus ratification, independent certification, accredited standards decision, or proof that the future v1.0 governance body has approved it.

The editor MUST disclose:

- the evidence and review considered;
- the change class;
- the decision and limitations;
- any material conflicts;
- the release or rollback route;
- whether review was advisory, independent, or self-attested.

External reviewers are being assembled. Their findings SHOULD be preserved and dispositioned, but a complete multi-stakeholder body is not a v0.x release prerequisite.

## v0.x amendment path

1. Discussion, issue, critique, implementation finding, or draft proposal.
2. Evidence-backed replacement text or artifact.
3. Classification as editorial, clarification, normative-compatible, breaking, constitutional, or security emergency.
4. Documented advisory or external review appropriate to the development risk.
5. Development Decision Record identifying the editor's disposition and the non-consensus status.
6. Pull request and validation.
7. Merge, changelog, and release-administration update.

Normative-compatible changes require a Development Decision Record. Rejected proposals remain preserved. Security emergencies may impose temporary fail-closed restrictions and require retrospective review.

## Non-self-certification

The Founder and Initial Editor may make developmental release decisions during v0.x but MUST NOT represent those decisions as independent approval, consensus ratification, certification, accreditation, or regulatory acceptance.

An implementation proposer may explain and revise its proposal. Claims of independent verification require a reviewer that is organizationally and operationally independent of the proposer.

## v1.0 governance gate

Before v1.0, GKOS MUST establish and publish:

- editor and committee appointment and removal rules;
- balanced stakeholder representation;
- voting, quorum, abstention, recusal, and dominance safeguards;
- public-review periods by change class;
- appeals and complaints procedures;
- interpretation and maintenance procedures;
- conflict-of-interest disclosures;
- release-signing and succession authority.

Only decisions made under that future governance model may be described as formal consensus ratifications.

## Canonical publication

The canonical v0.76 repository is `Odenknight/gkos-standard`. Migration requires a recorded development or governance decision and preservation of release history.
